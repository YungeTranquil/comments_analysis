Maryana
2.0 out of 5 stars The first book showed potential but...
Reviewed in the United States on January 13, 2019
We all know those rare books where every and each word is in its place, nothing to add or take away. Those are books that you savor slowly, without skipping a single phrase and never wish to end.
The "Court of Thorns and Roses" is not of them.
Yet I've read all three books because the story line is actually pretty good. I don't mind the author borrowing from other literary works one bit, I think she did a good job of weaving tried and true fantasy ingredients into something capable of existing on its own.
However, I really wish she took the time to polish it, give it her own unique and recognizable style. It felt rushed, lazy and sloppy enough that I, a rather fast reader to begin with, skimmed through pages, skipping most of the dialogues and only tracking the general plot, finishing three books in three days, glad for them to be over.
It's not worth your time to read them slowly and intently because:
1. The world Sarah J. Maas has created doesn't feel real enough. As if she sketched its contours but didn't care enough to color it thoroughly. The characters, some of them hundreds and thousands years old, all speak the same modern teenage jargon, that you might hear out in the street. And they act that way too, unfortunately, adding to the feel of two-dimensional cardboard figures.
Worse, a lot of phrases and actions are derivative so much that they feel mindlessly copypasted after a while. Was there an editor at all, I wonder? How can any editor worth its salt not ring an alarm, reading the same "vulgar gesture", "vomit", "eyes rolling" and hands endlessly "clenching into fists" over and over again. Just type those into your kindle search option and be amused.
2. The dialogues, while being cute at times, still feel forced, artificial and superfluous so much that many of them hardly add any depth to the story. A LOT of them could've been cut out without hurting the dynamics of the plot.
3. Sex scenes. I suppose the laurels of "50 Shades of Grey" kept the author from sleeping some nights, I mean all those frustrated housewives and awakening adolescents to tap into, how can one resist. Hey, I am not religious and I am not a prude, I enjoy artful erotica as much as anybody and I have to admit that Sarah J. Maas can paint a pretty hot picture. But c'mon, why so much and so explicitly raw? Making the reader's ears ring and blood boil with hinting, implying, dancing around the edges of the act, leaving the act to one's imagination —that's where true mastery lies, true grace. There's too much porn in this world already.

To conclude, I think Mrs. Maas is a promising author who can tell a hell of a story, however, I hope she won't rush it so much in the future, fearing a deadline maybe, but takes time to develop depth and elegance to her writing. I also hope she befriends a good editor.
