I was flipping through Documentary films on either my Amazon streaming or Netflix and came across the independent documentary film "Sriracha" and clicked play. Aside from being a totally delightful and engaging film it sure perked my interest or rather taste buds to try it.

My Spouse watched the movie and at his next trip to the market brought back a bottle, but alas the wrong kind a brand called Rand. Because the word "Sriracha" can't be trademarked there are countless sauces. If you plan to shop around here's two tips. The sauce has a big rooster on the bottle and chances are you won't find it in the condiment isle with ketchup and mustard - but rather the exotic or imported food section.

As far as hot sauce is concerned I'm not really a fan. A little Tabasco or Red Hot on Scrambled eggs and so on. I adore spicy foods and Put Wasabi on and into most anything but have found hot sauces, to be just that hot and one dimensional.

However as good as the Rand Brand was, boy does it pale in comparison to this wonderfully rich, complex an addicting sauce!

Frankly I was so "over saturated" with all the Sriracha fanatics I expected to be disappointed and just toss it off as a product that got lucky and over hyped. it isn't and it quite deserves every bit of it's continuing accolades and popularity.

Rich with garlic and a heat that you feel after not at first, it's more of a finishing note and please note a little as in very little goes a long , long way. It's also subtly sweet with kind of a Thai taste but it's impossible to pin point.

We're in love with it, we've made sticky Sriracha Ribs, Shrimp and sauces. It really does work with virtually everything and anything, I turned out the best chili I've ever made. Simply outstanding, if you haven't tried it go ahead you'll never regret it.

🔹 Catrinka 🔹
5.0 out of 5 stars Not your mama's Tabasco sauce
Reviewed in the United States on October 14, 2013
Verified Purchase
Our family is made up of hot sauce fanatics. One entire shelf in the door of our fridge is lined almost entirely with various hot sauces. My husband is the worst culprit. While on vacation, he usually manages to find some new locally-made variety that makes the trip home with us.

Out of ALL of this assortment, though, our hands-down favorite is Huy Fong Sriracha Hot Chili Sauce.

Because we do like a "hot" hot sauce, we always dismiss the milder forms of this condiment. Some hot sauces are more of a watery, hot-vinegar taste. Others have SO much heat to them that flavor can't actually be determined...all you can tell is that your mouth is on fire. That's what we love about this sriracha sauce--while hot, it also has a more interesting, full-flavor to it.

The Huy Fong sriracha is made of a purée of fresh red jalapeños, garlic powder, sugar, salt and vinegar. The first ingredient of many hot sauces is vinegar, or in some cases, even just water. The red jalapenos in this sriracha sauce are definitely hot, but with a bit of a fruity taste that is sweetened with the small amount of sugar it contains.

It's great as just a condiment on whatever food you like hot sauce on (which, in our family, is most foods). It's also a wonderful addition to many recipes. In a stir-fry, I'll use this sriracha as part of my heat base in the sauce. A small amount added to a cream sauce or mayonnaise dressing adds a lot of zing.

This Huy Fong Sriracha Hot Chili Sauce is generally found in the Top Ten in most published rankings of the best hot sauces...and many times it is in first place. I'd have to agree. This green-capped scorcher is #1 in our home.
